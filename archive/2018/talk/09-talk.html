Transient Execution Attacks: Meltdown, Spectre, and how to mitigate them

Daniel Gruß

In this talk, we will discuss transient execution attacks: Meltdown and Spectre. After understanding the key idea behind Meltdown and Spectre we will learn about more recent variants and discuss fundamental differences between Meltdown and Spectre. Afterwards we will discuss the various countermeasures against transient execution attacks. We will see that there is still a lot of work to be done until transient execution attacks are sufficiently mitigated.

Bio

Daniel Gruss (@lavados) is an Assistant Professor at Graz University of Technology. He finished his PhD with distinction in less than 3 years. He has been involved in teaching operating system undergraduate courses since 2010. Daniel's research focuses on software-based side-channel attacks that exploit timing differences in hardware and operating systems. He implemented the first remote fault attack running in a website, known as Rowhammer.js. He frequently speaks at top international venues, such as Black Hat, Usenix Security, IEEE S&P, ACM CCS, Chaos Communication Congress, and others. His research team was one of the four teams that found the Meltdown and Spectre bugs published in early 2018.
