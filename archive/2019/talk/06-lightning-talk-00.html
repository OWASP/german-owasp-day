Penetration testing in the context of IoT

Tanja Dyroff

Many Internet of Things devices are made for monitoring what matters most to us—our aparments, children and pets. We test security and privacy of several IoT cameras. First, we do a lot of penetration testing without following any guidelines and without using automated tools. These tests are tailored to each device’s technologies and outcomes are quite individual. Second, we do structured testing by following existing penetration testing guides. We compare the applicability, performance and quality of penetration testing guides with respect to finding vulnerabilities on devices in the wild.

Tanja Dyroff is a computer science (IT security) student from TU Darmstadt. She is currently writing her Master thesis in which penetration testing guides for the Internet of Things are tested on connected cameras.
