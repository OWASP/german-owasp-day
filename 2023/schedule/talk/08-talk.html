Better don't be too QUIC(K)

Yuri Gbur

The QUIC protocol is gaining more and more traction since its standardization in 2021 and the recent finalization of the HTTP3 standard which utilizes it as the core transport protocol. This has been leading to a rising interest by various big tech companies, developing new implementations. QUIC promises to make security and privacy a first-class citizen; yet, challenging these claims is of utmost importance. To this end, this presentation provides insights into challenges deploying QUIC and securing environments around it. We highlight shortcomings of current stateful firewalls and discuss bypass capabilities. Furthermore, we show client-side request forgery attacks that directly emerge from the QUIC protocol design and not from common vulnerabilities. We demonstrate that one of the uncovered attack modalities can be utilized to impersonate other UDP-based protocols, e.g., DNS requests. In addition, we identify traffic amplification vectors and pitfalls in the protocol specification which we confirm in our evaluation of 13 QUIC server implementations. Lastly, we discuss and propose mitigation approaches for the uncovered vulnerabilities.

Yuri Gbur arbeitet seit 2019 bei SEC Consult als Security Consultant und Researcher im Bereich Web, Mobile und Cloud. In Kollaboration mit SEC Consult hat er 2021 seine Masterarbeit an der TU Berlin in der Informatik zum Thema "Request Forgery in QUIC" abgeschlossen. Aus der Masterarbeit folgte ein Paper, welches auf dem renommierten NDSS Symposium 2023 vorgestellt wurde. Aktuell ist er Deputy Lead des Cloud-Security-Bereichs bei SEC Consult.
